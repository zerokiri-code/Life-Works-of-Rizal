Timeline of José Rizal’s Life

1892 - Rizal’s Exile in Dapitan

José Rizal arrives in Dapitan under Spanish orders, marking the beginning of his four-year exile. Though isolated from the country’s political movements, he quietly prepares to make an impact in his new community.

1892–1896 - José Rizal Life in Dapitan

Rizal leads a life of service in Dapitan. He establishes a school for young boys, builds a small hospital, and opens a farm. He uses his diverse skills to provide education, medicine, and engineering solutions to the town. His activities earn the respect of locals and foreign visitors alike. He also maintains communication with his family and fellow reformists, showing that he remains connected to the broader struggle for Philippine freedom.

1896 - Arrest of José Rizal

Rizal is summoned to return to Manila under the pretext of serving as a doctor in Cuba. Upon his arrival, he is arrested by Spanish authorities, accused of sedition and involvement with the revolutionary Katipunan movement—even though he advocated for peaceful reform

December 30, 1896 - Execution of José Rizal

Rizal is executed by firing squad in Luneta, Manila. His martyrdom stirs emotions across the nation, awakening a deeper desire for independence. His final act of bravery and his writings inspire generations to come. Even in death, Rizal continues to be a symbol of peaceful resistance and dedication to justice.
